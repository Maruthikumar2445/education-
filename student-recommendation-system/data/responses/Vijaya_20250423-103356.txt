**1. Mathematics (Foundation for AIML)**

*   **Resource 1: *Linear Algebra - Foundations to Frontiers (LAFF)* from edX (University of Texas at Austin)**
    *   **Description:** A highly regarded course providing a deep understanding of linear algebra concepts, which are crucial for machine learning.  It uses visual explanations and intuitive examples.
    *   **Estimated Time Commitment:** 8-10 hours per week (including lectures, exercises, and problem sets)
    *   **Link:** [https://www.edx.org/course/linear-algebra-foundations-to-frontiers](https://www.edx.org/course/linear-algebra-foundations-to-frontiers)
    *   **Cost:** Free to audit.  To earn a certificate: ~$149.
    *   **Why it fits:** LAFF is praised for its visual approach to teaching linear algebra, making abstract concepts more accessible. It covers the necessary mathematical underpinnings for AIML.  The structured format of the edX course provides the certification you desire.

*   **Resource 2: *Calculus: Single Variable Part 1 - Functions* from Coursera (University of Pennsylvania)**
    *   **Description:** Covers single variable Calculus with visual explanations and examples, essential for understanding optimization algorithms used in AIML.
    *   **Estimated Time Commitment:** 3-5 hours per week (including lectures, exercises, and problem sets)
    *   **Link:** [https://www.coursera.org/learn/calculus1](https://www.coursera.org/learn/calculus1)
    *   **Cost:** Coursera Plus subscription (~$59/month) might be more economical if you plan to take multiple courses.  Individual courses can also be purchased.
    *   **Why it fits:** Penn's course on Coursera uses examples and visualization to explain concepts that can often be difficult to grasp from textbooks alone. The course also allows for a certification to be earned.

*   **Resource 3: *3Blue1Brown Essence of Linear Algebra* (YouTube Series)**
    *   **Description:** A visually stunning YouTube series that provides an intuitive understanding of linear algebra.  It complements the more formal LAFF course.
    *   **Estimated Time Commitment:** 2-3 hours per week (watching videos and doing supplementary exercises)
    *   **Link:** [https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab)
    *   **Cost:** Free
    *   **Why it fits:** The series is purely visual and focuses on building intuition, making it perfect for your learning style.  It solidifies the concepts from the LAFF course in a different, highly memorable way.
