Okay, here's a personalized learning plan tailored to your preferences, focusing on Advanced AIML with a visual learning style and a strong emphasis on Mathematics and Computer Science foundations:

**Overall Strategy:**

This plan prioritizes building a solid theoretical foundation in mathematics and computer science alongside practical AIML skills. **1. Mathematics (Foundation for AIML):**

*   **Resource 1: "Linear Algebra for Machine Learning" by Imperial College London (Coursera)**

    *   **Description:** This course covers essential linear algebra concepts like vectors, matrices, eigenvalues, and eigenvectors with a focus on their applications in machine learning. Uses visual explanations and Jupyter notebooks for hands-on practice.
    *   **Why it fits:** Visual explanations of abstract concepts, hands-on coding exercises, and clear connection to ML make it perfect for visual learners.
    *   **Estimated Time Commitment:** 4-6 hours per week
    *   **Link:** [https://www.coursera.org/learn/linear-algebra-machine-learning](https://www.coursera.org/learn/linear-algebra-machine-learning)
    *   **Cost:** Free to audit, Coursera Plus subscription or individual course purchase for graded assignments and certificate. (Coursera Plus: ~ $59/month)

*   **Resource 2: "Mathematics for Machine Learning: Multivariate Calculus" by Imperial College London (Coursera)**

    *   **Description:**  Extends your calculus knowledge to multiple variables. Essential for understanding optimization algorithms in deep learning. Includes visualizations and coding exercises.
    *   **Why it fits:** Similar to the Linear Algebra course, visually oriented and connects directly to ML.
    *   **Estimated Time Commitment:** 4-6 hours per week
    *   **Link:** [https://www.coursera.org/learn/multivariate-calculus-machine-learning](https://www.coursera.org/learn/multivariate-calculus-machine-learning)
    *   **Cost:** Free to audit, Coursera Plus subscription or individual course purchase for graded assignments and certificate. (Coursera Plus: ~ $59/month)

*   **Resource 3: 3Blue1Brown's "Essence of Linear Algebra" and "Essence of Calculus" (YouTube)**

    *   **Description:** A series of videos that provide an extremely intuitive and visually appealing understanding of linear algebra and calculus concepts.
    *   **Why it fits:** Purely visual and focuses on conceptual understanding rather than rigorous proofs. Great as a supplement to the Coursera courses.
    *   **Estimated Time Commitment:** 2-3 hours per week (total for both series)
    *   **Link:**
        *   Linear Algebra: [https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab)
        *   Calculus: [https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab)
    *   **Cost:** Free

*   **Resource 4: "Probability and Statistics" (MIT OpenCourseWare)**

    *   **Description:** A comprehensive course on probability and statistics, covering topics such as probability distributions, hypothesis testing, and regression.
    *   **Why it fits:** Free resource that provides a solid foundation for understanding statistical concepts in machine learning.
    *   **Estimated Time Commitment:** 3-4 hours per week
    *   **Link:** [https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/mathematics/18-05-introduction-to-probability-and-statistics-spring-2014/](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/mathematics/18-05-introduction-to-probability-and-statistics-spring-2014/)

**Suggested Schedule (19 hours per week):**

*   **Weekdays (approx. 2 hours/day):**
    *   30 mins - Linear Algebra or Calculus (Coursera/YouTube)
    *   30 mins - Probability/Statistics (MIT OCW)
    *   1 hour - TensorFlow or Deep Learning Specialization (Coursera/Fast.ai)

*   **Weekend (approx. 4-5 hours/day):**
    *   1 hour - Data Structures and Algorithms (Coursera)
    *   1 hour - "Designing Data-Intensive Applications" or "Effective Modern C++" (Reading/Exercises)
    *   2-3 hours - TensorFlow or Deep Learning Specialization (Coursera/Fast.ai), dedicated project time.  Rotate each weekend between different components.

**Important Considerations for the Schedule:**

*   **Flexibility:**  This is a template. Adjust based on your energy levels and deadlines.  If you find a particular topic fascinating, spend more time on it.
*   **Breaks:**  Take regular breaks! Use the Pomodoro Technique (25 minutes work, 5 minutes break) to stay focused.
*   **Prioritization:** If a deadline for an assignment is approaching for a particular course, prioritize that.
*   **Project Time:**  The most important part of learning AIML is actually *doing* projects.  Dedicate a significant portion of your weekend time to working on personal projects or projects suggested in the courses.
